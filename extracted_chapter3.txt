390: CHAPTER THREE
391: RESEARCH METHODOLOGY
392: 3.1 Preamble
393: This chapter presents the methodology adopted for the development and evaluation of the EduPredict AI system, which is designed to predict students’ academic performance using machine learning techniques. The chapter explains the research design, population of the study, sampling technique, data collection instrument, preprocessing procedures, and methods of analysis used in the study. It also discusses the machine learning algorithms implemented and the deployment architecture of the system. In addition, ethical considerations such as data privacy, anonymization, and bias mitigation are highlighted to ensure responsible use of the predictive system. The methodology adopted ensures that the developed model is reliable, accurate, and suitable for assisting educators in identifying at-risk students and improving academic decision-making.
394: 3.2 Research Design
395: This study adopted a quantitative predictive research design. The quantitative approach was selected because the study relies on numerical and structured data for training machine learning algorithms. Predictive research design is suitable because the aim of the study is to forecast students’ academic outcomes based on historical academic and demographic data. The design enables the identification of patterns and relationships between independent variables such as attendance, study hours, and previous grades, and the dependent variable, which is academic performance status. Furthermore, the predictive design supports the use of statistical and computational techniques to evaluate the effectiveness of different machine learning models in educational prediction tasks.

397: 3.2.1 Justification for Research Design
398: The predictive research design was chosen because the study focuses on forecasting future academic performance using existing student records. Machine learning models require historical datasets to identify hidden relationships and trends within data, making predictive design highly appropriate for this research. The design also supports data-driven decision-making by allowing the system to generate insights that can help educators provide early interventions to struggling students. In addition, quantitative predictive research enhances objectivity since the results are based on measurable variables and computational analysis rather than subjective opinions. The approach also improves the reliability and reproducibility of the research findings.
399: 3.3 Population of the Study
400: The population of this study comprises undergraduate students in tertiary institutions whose academic records and demographic information are represented in the dataset used for the research. The population includes students with different academic backgrounds, socioeconomic conditions, and levels of academic performance. This diversity ensures that the predictive model learns from a broad range of student profiles and behaviors. The study population is important because it provides the basis for understanding factors that influence academic success or failure. By using a representative dataset, the developed system can make more generalized predictions applicable to a larger student population.
401: 3.3.1 Dataset as Population Representation
402: The student_data.csv dataset serves as a representative sample of the study population. The dataset contains records of students with varying academic performances and demographic characteristics. It includes important variables such as age, gender, attendance percentage, study hours, socioeconomic status, continuous assessment scores, and previous term grades. These features provide sufficient information for training and evaluating the machine learning models. The dataset also reflects different categories of academic performance, thereby improving the predictive capability of the EduPredict AI system.
403: 3.4 Sample and Sampling Technique
404: The sample for this study was obtained from the available student dataset used in training the machine learning models. The dataset was divided into subsets for model training and evaluation to ensure unbiased testing of the algorithms. Sampling techniques are essential in machine learning research because they help improve fairness and reduce bias during model training. The selected sample contained sufficient records to allow the algorithms learn meaningful patterns and relationships. Proper sampling also ensures that the predictive performance of the developed system is reliable and generalizable.
405: 3.4.1 Data Partitioning
406: The dataset was partitioned into training and testing sets to facilitate model development and evaluation. Eighty percent (80%) of the dataset was allocated to the training set, while twenty percent (20%) was reserved for testing purposes. The training set was used to train the machine learning algorithms, while the testing set was used to evaluate their predictive accuracy on unseen data. This partitioning strategy helps to prevent overfitting and ensures that the models can generalize effectively to new student records. The data partitioning process was implemented using the train_test_split() function available in the Scikit-learn library.
407: Figure 3.2 Presents the Distribution of the Dataset into training and testing subsets used during Model Development
408: 3.4.2 Sampling Method
409: A stratified random sampling technique was employed in splitting the dataset into training and testing sets. Stratified sampling ensures that each class of the target variable is proportionally represented in both subsets of the dataset. This method was chosen because it reduces the risk of class imbalance, which could negatively affect the predictive performance of the machine learning models. The use of stratified sampling also improves fairness and consistency during evaluation. As a result, the developed EduPredict AI system can produce more balanced and reliable predictions across different categories of student performance.
410: 3.5 Instrument for Data Collection
411: The primary instrument for data collection in this study is the historical student dataset (student_data.csv). The dataset contains structured academic and demographic information relevant to predicting students’ academic performance. Unlike traditional studies that use questionnaires or interviews, this research relies on secondary educational data for analysis and prediction. The dataset was carefully selected because it contains variables closely associated with student performance in academic environments. Using a structured dataset allows the machine learning models to process information efficiently and generate meaningful predictions.

449: 3.5.1 Description of Dataset Features
450: The dataset contains seven input features and one target variable. The input features include Age, Gender, Socioeconomic Status, Attendance Percentage, Study Hours Per Week, Previous Term Grade, and Continuous Assessment Score. These variables were selected because they have significant influence on students’ academic outcomes according to educational research. The target variable is Performance_Status, which represents the academic performance category to be predicted by the system. Together, these features provide sufficient information for the machine learning algorithms to learn patterns associated with student achievement.
451: 3.6 Validity and Reliability of the Instrument
452: The validity and reliability of the EduPredict AI system were established through the use of standard machine learning evaluation techniques. Validity refers to the ability of the predictive model to accurately measure and predict students’ academic performance. Reliability refers to the consistency of the model in generating accurate predictions across different datasets. To ensure reliability, multiple machine learning algorithms were trained and evaluated using the same dataset and preprocessing techniques. The use of performance metrics such as Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and F1-Score further strengthened the reliability and validity of the developed system.
453: 3.6.1 Performance Evaluation Metrics
454: The machine learning models were evaluated using standard classification metrics. The evaluation metrics used in this study are;
455: Accuracy was used to determine the proportion of correctly predicted instances out of the total predictions made by the model. 
456: Precision measures the number of correctly predicted positive observations relative to all predicted positive observations. 
457: Recall evaluates the model’s ability to correctly identify all actual positive cases within the dataset. 
458: The F1-Score was also used because it provides a balanced measure of Precision and Recall, especially in situations where class imbalance may exist. These metrics collectively helped determine the best-performing algorithm for deployment in the EduPredict AI system.
459: In addition, a confusion matrix was used to visualize the classification performance of the machine learning models by showing correctly and incorrectly classified instances across different categories of student performance. The confusion matrix helped provide deeper insight into the predictive capability of each algorithm beyond the standard evaluation metrics.
460: 3.7 Method of Data Collection (Data Preprocessing)
461: The dataset used in this research is secondary data sourced from the Kaggle open-source repository. The dataset titled “Student Performance Prediction Dataset” was selected because it contains relevant academic and demographic variables suitable for machine learning-based educational prediction tasks. Secondary data was selected because it provides an efficient and reliable means of obtaining structured educational data suitable for machine learning applications. After obtaining the dataset, preprocessing operations were performed to prepare the data for model training and analysis. Data preprocessing is essential because raw datasets often contain categorical values, inconsistent formats, and scaling differences that may reduce model performance. The preprocessing stage ensured that the dataset was clean, standardized, and suitable for machine learning implementation.
462: 3.7.1 Preprocessing Techniques
463: Several preprocessing techniques were implemented in preparing the dataset for machine learning analysis. The Preprocessing techniques implemented in this study are;
464: Label Encoding was applied to categorical variables such as Gender and the target variable (Performance_Status) to convert them into numerical representations understandable by machine learning algorithms. 
465: Ordinal Encoding was used for the Socioeconomic_Status feature to preserve its hierarchical structure, where categories such as Low, Medium, and High were assigned ordered numerical values. 
466: Feature Scaling was performed using the StandardScaler technique to standardize numerical features such as attendance percentage and study hours. This process ensured that variables with larger numerical ranges did not dominate the learning process of the algorithms. Overall, the preprocessing techniques improved the efficiency, accuracy, and consistency of the machine learning models.

468: 3.7.2 Feature Engineering
469: Feature engineering was carried out to improve the predictive capability of the machine learning models. Correlation analysis was used to examine the relationships between input variables and the target variable. Features that contributed significantly to academic performance prediction were retained for model training. Domain knowledge from educational studies was also considered in selecting and refining the dataset features. The feature engineering process helped improve model performance by ensuring that only relevant and meaningful variables were used during training and evaluation.
470: One limitation of the dataset is that it may not fully represent all categories of students across different institutions and geographical regions. However, preprocessing techniques and stratified sampling methods were applied to improve the reliability, fairness, and generalizability of the predictive models.
471: 3.8 Method of Data Analysis
472: The method of data analysis involved the use of machine learning classification techniques to train and evaluate predictive models. After preprocessing the dataset, the algorithms were trained using the training subset and evaluated using the testing subset. Comparative analysis was conducted to determine the best-performing model based on evaluation metrics. The analysis process was implemented using Python programming language and the Scikit-learn machine learning library. The overall aim of the analysis was to identify the most accurate and reliable algorithm for predicting student academic performance.
473: 3.8.1 Machine Learning Algorithms
474: The study implemented four machine learning algorithms: Logistic Regression, Support Vector Machine (SVM), Gradient Boosting Classifier, and Random Forest Classifier. Logistic Regression served as the baseline model for binary and multiclass classification tasks. Support Vector Machine was selected because of its effectiveness in handling high-dimensional datasets and classification boundaries. Gradient Boosting Classifier was implemented because it improves prediction accuracy by combining multiple weak learners sequentially. Random Forest Classifier was also used because of its ability to reduce overfitting and improve prediction stability through ensemble learning techniques.
475: Figure 3.4 illustrate the machine learning training and evaluation process adopted in this study
476: 3.8.2 Model Training and Selection Process
477: During the training process, each algorithm was trained using the preprocessed training dataset. The models were then evaluated on the testing dataset using Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and F1-Score metrics. Comparative analysis was conducted to identify the algorithm with the highest predictive performance. The best-performing model was selected as the final prediction model for deployment within the EduPredict AI system. Model serialization was performed using the joblib library to save the trained model and preprocessing objects for future use.
478: 3.8.3 System Development Architecture
479: The EduPredict AI system was deployed using a modern web-based architecture consisting of a FastAPI backend and a Vue.js 3 frontend. FastAPI was used to build the RESTful API responsible for handling prediction requests and returning model outputs efficiently. Vue.js 3 was used in developing the frontend interface because of its responsiveness, flexibility, and component-based structure. The deployment architecture allows users such as lecturers and academic advisors to interact with the prediction system through a user-friendly dashboard. The architecture also supports real-time prediction generation and seamless integration between the machine learning backend and the web interface.

481: 3.8.4 Software and Development Tools
482: The development of the EduPredict AI system was carried out using several software tools and frameworks. The software tools used for the development of the system include;
483: Python programming language was used for implementing the machine learning models because of its extensive support for data science and artificial intelligence applications. The Scikit-learn library was used for preprocessing, model training, testing, and evaluation. Pandas and NumPy libraries were used for data manipulation and numerical computations respectively. 
484: FastAPI was used in developing the backend API responsible for handling prediction requests and communication with the trained model. 
485: Vue.js 3 and Tailwind CSS were used for designing the frontend user interface due to their responsiveness and component-based architecture. Visual Studio Code served as the primary development environment, while the Joblib library was used for model serialization and storage.
486: 3.9 Ethical Considerations
487: Ethical considerations were carefully observed throughout the development and deployment of the EduPredict AI system. Since educational datasets may contain sensitive information, measures were taken to ensure privacy, confidentiality, and responsible use of the data. Ethical practices were also necessary to reduce bias and ensure fairness in the predictive process. The study emphasized the use of the system as a supportive educational tool rather than a means of discrimination or punishment. Adhering to ethical principles improved the trustworthiness and acceptability of the developed system.
488: 3.9.1 Data Privacy and Anonymization
489: The dataset used in this research was anonymized to protect students’ personal information. Personally identifiable information such as names, matriculation numbers, and addresses were excluded from the dataset before analysis. This ensured compliance with data privacy principles and reduced the risk of exposing sensitive student information. The anonymization process also improved the ethical integrity of the study by ensuring that predictions could not be linked to specific individuals. Data security measures were also considered during storage and processing of the dataset.
490: 3.9.2 Bias Mitigation
491: Bias mitigation strategies were implemented to reduce unfairness within the predictive models. Stratified sampling was used to ensure balanced representation of performance categories during training and testing. Multiple machine learning algorithms were compared to reduce dependence on a single biased model. The preprocessing and evaluation stages were carefully monitored to identify and minimize potential sources of algorithmic bias. These measures improved the fairness and reliability of the EduPredict AI system across different student groups.
492: 3.9.3 Responsible Use of the System
493: The EduPredict AI system was designed strictly as a decision-support tool for educators and academic advisors. The predictions generated by the system are intended to assist in identifying students who may require academic support or intervention. The system is not intended to replace human judgment or to stigmatize students based on predicted outcomes. Responsible use of the system ensures that predictions are interpreted carefully and used to promote positive educational outcomes. Ultimately, the system aims to enhance academic support services and improve student success rates in tertiary institutions.
